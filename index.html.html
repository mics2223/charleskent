Information of the Song

  • "My Heart Will Go On" is about the undying love between Rose and Jack from the movie Titanic, a love so strong that it transcends even death and distance.
  • it was released in 1997
  • From the perspective of an elderly Rose, the lyrics express the enduring nature of their bond, the continuation of life, and the belief in a spiritual connection that keeps their love alive and assures a future reunion beyond the physical world.
  • "My Heart Will Go On" was written by composer James Horner and lyricist Will Jennings as the theme for the 1997 film Titanic
  • Celine Dion, after some persuasion from her husband and manager, René Angélil, recorded a demo of the song, which was then presented to Cameron and approved. The resulting song became a massive global hit, winning an Academy Award and multiple Grammys, and remains Dion's signature song.
  • "My Heart Will Go On" is a song performed by Canadian singer Celine Dion, used as the theme for the 1997 film Titanic. It was composed by James Horner, with lyrics by Will Jennings, and produced by Horner, Walter Afanasieff and Simon Franglen.[1][2] It was released as a single internationally by Columbia and Epic on November 24, 1997, and included on Dion's album Let's Talk About Love (1997) and the Titanic soundtrack.
